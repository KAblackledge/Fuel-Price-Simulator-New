Discussion Questions

  1. When fuel prices double, the break-even fare jumps sharply. Why can't Cotswold Airways just always raise prices to match — especially when every competitor faces the same cost rise?
  2. British Airways hedges 60–70% of its fuel needs in advance. If prices then crash (as in COVID 2020), hedging costs billions. Is it still worth doing, and why?
  3. Heathrow charges £23.73 per passenger in airport fees alone. Why do airlines still prefer LHR over cheaper airports despite this cost?
  4. If jet fuel stayed above $4.00/gal permanently, how might long-haul aviation look different in 20 years? Consider routes, aircraft technology and alternatives to flying.
  5. In April 2020 fuel hit $0.61/gal — the cheapest in history — yet airlines were in crisis. What does this tell us about the relationship between fuel costs and airline profitability?
